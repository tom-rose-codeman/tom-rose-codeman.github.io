Culture 2

What is your long term goal and or career pathway?

My long term goal is to develop the skills to be able to make a living though coding/web development, and eventually possibly use those skills to be able to work overseas.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I would like to see myself develop perseverance to be able to commit time to something for long periods of time. also, to be able to talk to people I don't know better.

A description of your own strengths and limitations

I like to know and understand information so when I can get interesting information about something I will try to, but I spend too long on something I start to struggle and my productivity slows.

Based on the above description, a commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include - an explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives

I will be nice to my fellow learners as well as the facilitators and other people that will be at the course. I will also ask for help when I need it. I will focus periods of time to get the work done and will have breaks to refresh the mind at regular intervals. I will also go to be at reasonable times as to get a good amount of sleep. I will also go over problems again formulaic if I don't understand them.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

I will seek help by going and finding a member of my cohort and asking them for help by telling them the problem I have. If we both don't know ill ask another member or I will approach A facilitator for help.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

I expect the facilitators to guide me on the correct learning path to be able to learn the skills to be a web developer. I also expect the facilitators to assist me with any problems I have, but give me the opportunity to solve the problems on my own without the facilitator spoon-feeding me the answers.
