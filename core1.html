An ethical Decision

Around two years ago when I was still in college or just left college, I cant remember. a friend ended up messaging me and asked me if I could borrow my mums credit card, which I said no do, then I told my mum who told my friends mum so my friend got in pretty big trough I believe when someone places trust in me to do(or not do something In this situation) that I should uphold it, unless its something that puts a lot of strain on me, which not telling someone credit card numbers will do. I suppose valuing a friend and trying to help them is a possible decision but ive found that losing someone that you care about's trust hurts and does more harm in than the possible benefit of helping someone by betraying someone that trust you. I also value not stealing as its not fair to the person who's stolen, and im not sure if my friend would've ever returned the money if I gave him the credit card, so that went into my decision process.

Not giving my friend my mums credit card was an easy decsion to make, I didnt even have to really think about it. Weighing my values in my head I knew that there would be a net large negative on both myself and my mum if I was to do it. Another thing altogether though was me deciding to tell my mum. I ended up telling my mum because of the potential to make my friend a better person and I guess give him a wakeup call. I try to reduce harm wherever I can if its easy for me and in this situation it was. Of course there was a bit of a little bit of animosity between us a little while but we ended up still being friends after and as far as im aware he hasnt done something similar since.
